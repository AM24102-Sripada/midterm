Projects

Enhanced superconductivity in Y1Ba2Cu3O7-y (YBCO): Targeted doping of ZnO in Y211 and LP pellets (Ongoing Project)

Abstract: This research presents a comprehensive and contemporary study investigating the impact of Zn (Zinc) doping on the superconducting properties of Y1Ba2Cu3O7-y (YBCO). By addressing the limited modern studies on Zn doping effects on YBCO, our novel study aims at using contemporary TSIG (Top Seeded Infiltration Growth) process to fabricate high performance superconductors and study the effect of different doping pathways of Zn through addition in Y211 pellet and LP pellet separately, allowing precise understanding. Through meticulous microstructural, magnetic and electrical characterization, this study aims to establish optimized doping strategies, providing crucial insights into YBCO superconducting properties for high-performance applications.

Fabrication & Characterization of YBa2Cu3Oy Superconductor using TSIG process

Summary: This project focused on fabricating and characterizing a high-temperature superconductor, YBa2Cu3Oy (YBCO), using the Top-Seeded Infiltration Growth (TSIG) process. Superconductors offer exciting possibilities for energy-efficient power transmission, advanced medical imaging (like smaller MRIs), and new transportation systems (like Maglev trains). The TSIG method was chosen to create a high-quality material with improved strength and consistent properties. My work resulted in a YBCO superconductor capable of trapping a strong magnetic field (3000 Gauss or 0.3 Tesla), demonstrating its excellent performance at a critical temperature of 92.2 Kelvin. It was found to be capable of transporting 62,500 or 62.5 kA per cm2 cross section.
